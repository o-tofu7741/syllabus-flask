キーワード
Critical Discourse Analysis, Power and Knowledge, Distributed Cognition, Time, Space, Research Ethics
授業科目の概要
This course aims to increase students' knowledge of data analysis validation, research, references, and structure of the overall research project presentation. Students are supposed to bring their new work every week. This course review the existing thesis from different perspectives. Suggestion for revisions will be given in class. When students make a presentation in the conference they can bring their work previously. Working papers for publication opportunities are also reviewed in class. In case of multiple students take this course, peer reviews and peer critiques are practiced. This course relates to the diploma policy, 1 and 6.
本授業の到達目標
The ultimate goal of the course is to complete a doctoral thesis. To achieve this goal, the following sub goals are set: 1) Sufficient contextual information including historical development and the current situation of the subject matter, are provided in the beginning of the thesis. 2) A clear and adequate research questions are presented. 3) Thorough explication and reflection on the theoretical or conceptual frame are discussed. 4) When multiple theories and concepts are presented, interrelation of the theories and concepts are discussed. 5) Thick description of the research site, participants, and environments are provided. 6) Rationales of research methodology employed in the study is elucidated. 7) Data are presented in a theoretically relevant manner. 8) Analyses are valid and supported by data. 9) Conclusion includes future direction of research in the field.

授業計画
事前事後学修内容
1
The introduction: The issues paid attention to in the study. Historical, environmental, situated contexts and how they influence the issue.
Writing, reviewing and examining the introduction part of the doctoral dissertation.
2
The context of the study. A determinant factor of research questions. Context includes historical factors, environmental factors, political factors, economical factors and other relevant factors that surround the issue.
Writing, reviewing and examining the contexts that influence the research
3
The background reasons for research questions need to be clearly presented in the beginning of the chapter. The importance of the research question is conveyed by providing the reasons.
Reflection on the backgrounds and contexts that foreground the research questions
4
How to form research questions are discussed in this session. Often research questions are based on the researchers' interpretation of the situation. How bias are minimized in forming questions are discussed.
Reexamination of the research question(s)
5
The reciprocal relation between data analysis and selection of concepts and theories as frameworks of the data analysis is discussed in this meeting.
Revisiting the conceptual or theoretical frameworks referring to the data analyzed
6
In this meeting, concepts such as power and knowledge in discourse, distributed and extended cognition, multi-sensoriality of time and space are discussed.
Reading of studies that are referenced in the conceptual and theoretical framework chapter.
7
Inter-relation between power and knowledge, and extended cognition is discussed based on previous arguments in past research is discussed. Furthermore, how the notion of time and space is related to the above two concepts is discussed.
Examination of past research for exploring inter-relation between power and knowldge
8
研究倫理教育Research ethics is the main focus on this session. Ways to protect participants' privacy and feeling are shown from past research projects. How to provide detailed information of the research project with participants is instructed.
Extended and holistic examination of research ethics from multiple perspectives
9
In this session, ethical consideration of a research project with an organization is provided. we discuss a case that involve organizations is discussed. Consents from the corporate managers and individual employees in addition to human resource managers are discussed.
Reflecting on ethical issues for the participants.
10
This session focuses on validation in describing research. In order to assure validity of data analysis, how to provide data collection method is discussed. Triangulation of data collection methods and data source is explained with examples.
Reviewing validation methods in qualitative research
11
Peer debriefing, long term engagement, and other ways are discussed as means of maintaining validity of data analysis. Differences of the notion of validity and reliability is explicated.
Examination of peer reviewing eliciting data from the past reviews from publication opportunities and conferences
12
Three kinds of critical discourse approach are explained. The linguistic approach, historical approach, and cognitive approach is discussed. In particular, this session emphasizes the cognitive approach.
Literature reviews of critical discourse analysis
13
Concepts of power and critical discourse analysis as a method is discussed in this session. How to detect power in the context of cognitive activities is discussed.
Writing and reviewing the methodological discussion
14
This session suggests several recommended order of presenting theories and concepts as frameworks of the researcher's data analysis. A special focus on the inter-theoretical argument
Reexamination of presentation orders by thinking of alternative orders
15
This session reviews past research projects in the field and show several factors that are relevant in conclusion. Furthermore, critical reflection, grounded theory, and future research views are discussed.
Review and reexamine the whole dissertation again including references cited

成績評価基準
Students' achievement is evaluated by examining their thesis in terms of the goals of this course. Constant progress of thesis is an important factor for evaluation. Originality of the research and creative analysis contribute to positive evaluation as well. Understanding of research ethics is assessed in reference to writing of research ethics in the adequate part. Participation and reflective report of conference presentation are added to the over all evaluation when relevant. References listed are also evaluated in terms of completeness and accuracy following American Psychological Association (APA) manual. Careful attention should be paid to plagiarism and citation.

テキスト等、学修のために購入・準備が必要なもの
Textbooks and materials are informed during the course. As the contents of the thesis is not pre-determined in advance of the course. It is not possible to accurately predict textbooks and references prior to grasp the development of the thesis. However, participants are required to give a progress report of the doctoral thesis at every meeting. The supervisor will recommend readings and online lectures of relevant academic referring to the progress and contents of the doctoral thesis. In other words, students' thesis in progress is critical.
