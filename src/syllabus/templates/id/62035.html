Human Resource Development. Socio-economical factors, Curriculum Development, Digitalization effects, Globalization Effucts, Research Ethics
授業科目の概要
This course provides discussions on Human Resource Development in Japan. The lectures introduces thesocio historicadl influences to the development of Japanese Human Resource Development and Management including philosophical influences Japanese traditions, its colonialism, and post WWII changes.
本授業の到達目標
Students are expected to reach holistic understanding of Japanese HRM systems in various historical periods.

授業計画
事前事後学修内容
1
Overview of Japanese HRD/HRM system, past, present and future.
Collecting available resources, students prepare to talk about their views and evaluation of Japanese HRD/HRM system. This will give the lecturer to know the knowledge level of students
2
Historical factors. HRD in Edo era
Students search for relavant material and prepare for the topic of the day, HRD in Edo era
3
HRD and the meiji restoration
Students prepare for discussing socio-political changes caused by Meiji Restoration
4
Research Ethics
Collect information on Research Ethics for researchers
5
HRD and colonialism in Japan
Prepare to talk about Japanese colonialism
6
World War II and Japan's Education Policy
Prepare to talk about the political and economic background of the WW II in the Pacific area
7
Post WW II education policy
Read articles on Traditional Morality and Democratization
8
Oral Presentation
Prepare for interim report
9
HRD and Sharp economic growth
Prepare to discuss Japanese HRM formed after WWII
10
Economic Growth based on Lean Management
Prepare to discuss Kaizen and Just in Time in relation to HRD
11
Communities of Practice and Talent Management
Prepare to discuss Wenger's notion of Communities of Practice
12
New Economy and HRM
Readings of New Literacy Studies
13
AI and Competency Attribute
Prepare to discuss how AI influences required attributes of employees
14
Assessment
Prepare to discuss different ways of assessment of training and development programs
15
oral report
Prepare to present learning from the course
